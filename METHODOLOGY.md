# Research Methodology

## 1. Research Methodology
The methodology for this research is primarily qualitative, focusing on understanding the underlying factors influencing the settlement analysis within the specified context.

## 2. Data Sources
- **Primary Data**: Surveys conducted with stakeholders in the settlement areas.
- **Secondary Data**: Government reports, academic papers, and existing databases related to settlement patterns and trends.

## 3. Assumptions
- The data collected through surveys accurately represents the views and behaviors of the stakeholders.
- Trends observed in the secondary data continue to hold true.

## 4. Metrics Definitions
- **Settlement Efficiency**: The ratio of resources utilized in the settlement process to the total resources available.
- **Stakeholder Satisfaction Index**: A composite score derived from survey responses measuring satisfaction levels.

## 5. Limitations
- The reliance on self-reported data may introduce biases.
- External factors not accounted for during analysis may affect the results.

## 6. Conclusion
This document serves as a foundational reference for understanding the dynamics of the settlement analysis conducted in this research.
